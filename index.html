About Jane

Jane Doe is an award-winning composer and pianist whose work spans orchestral, chamber, and solo compositions, blending classical traditions with contemporary innovations.

Born in Vienna and trained at the prestigious Juilliard School, Jane has composed for leading orchestras worldwide. Her compositions are known for their emotional depth and innovative harmonies that push the boundaries of classical music while honoring its rich traditions.

"
Jane Doe's Symphony No. 1 establishes her as one of the most important compositional voices of her generation.
— The New York Times

As a pianist, Jane regularly performs her own compositions at major venues including Carnegie Hall and Royal Albert Hall. She has received numerous accolades including the Pulitzer Prize for Music and multiple Grammy nominations.

Musical Works

Jane's compositions span a diverse range of forms and ensembles, from intimate solo piano pieces to grand orchestral works. Her music is characterized by emotional depth, technical precision, and a unique harmonic language that bridges classical traditions with contemporary innovations.

Symphony No. 1 "Awakening"

2022

A four-movement symphony exploring themes of rebirth and transformation, premiered by the London Philharmonic Orchestra.

Concerto for Violin and Orchestra

2020

A virtuosic concerto written for violinist Sarah Chang, blending romantic sensibilities with contemporary techniques.

Oceanic Overture

2019

A tone poem inspired by the power and mystery of the ocean, commissioned by the San Francisco Symphony.

Celestial Variations

2018

A set of orchestral variations based on ancient astronomical concepts, premiered at the BBC Proms.

String Quartet No. 3 "Reflections"

2021

A quartet exploring themes of memory and time, commissioned by the Kronos Quartet.

Piano Trio in G Minor

2019

A three-movement work blending classical forms with jazz-influenced harmonies.

Wind Quintet "Forest Whispers"

2017

A programmatic quintet depicting the sounds and atmosphere of ancient forests.

Brass Sextet "Urban Rhythms"

2016

A dynamic work inspired by the patterns and energy of city life.

Piano Sonata No. 2 "Moonlight Echoes"

2022

A sonata in dialogue with Beethoven's "Moonlight" sonata, reimagining its themes in a contemporary context.

Twelve Preludes for Piano

2020

A cycle of preludes exploring all 12 major and minor keys, inspired by Bach and Chopin.

Nocturnes: Five Night Pieces

2018

A collection of atmospheric pieces exploring different aspects of night and darkness.

Variations on a Theme by Clara Schumann

2017

A set of variations honoring the legacy of Clara Schumann and female composers throughout history.

Song Cycle: "Ancient Voices"

2021

Settings of ancient poetry from diverse cultures for soprano and piano.

Choral Symphony "Unity"

2019

A large-scale work for orchestra and chorus exploring themes of human connection.

Three Shakespeare Songs

2017

Settings of Shakespeare's sonnets for baritone and chamber ensemble.

Opera: "The Clockmaker's Dream"

2015

A chamber opera based on a surrealist short story, premiered at the Aspen Music Festival.

Teaching

Jane is a dedicated educator who shares her knowledge and passion for music with students around the world.

Currently a Professor of Composition at the Juilliard School, Jane also gives masterclasses at leading conservatories and universities worldwide. Her teaching philosophy emphasizes the development of each student's unique voice while providing a strong foundation in compositional techniques and traditions.

Composition Lessons

Private instruction for advanced students and emerging composers, focusing on developing individual style and technical mastery.

Masterclasses

Group sessions exploring compositional techniques, analysis, and the creative process, available for booking at institutions worldwide.

Workshops

Intensive multi-day programs covering specific aspects of composition, from orchestration to writing for specific instruments or ensembles.
